The Great Debate

By Conor Organ. 27/4/2016

The day of the great debate arrived, we were prepared and nervous. The research had been done and we gathered in the crit room for some heated arguments.

For me and Thomas’ debate we were against the motion “The Design Brief limits Creativity”. I went first and based my argument on Phil Hansen.

Phil Hansen is a multimedia artist who was 3 years into an Arts course when he injured his hand and developed permanent nerve damage in his hand and has to live with a shake for the rest of his life. This wouldn’t normally be a problem but Phil had specialised in pointillism which is art using dots and requires a steady hand. This was a major limit on Phil’s creativity, so much so he spent 3 years doing everything but work.

After the 3 years was up he went back to art and tried multiple other art forms because he couldn’t use his pointillism skills. He used a wide variety of other art forms like blowtorches, sculptures and using his feet to create art.

“It was then that I thought back to this limitation of my jittery hand,”“I realised, if I ever wanted my creativity back, I had to quit trying so hard to look outside of the box, and get back into it.”

Coming back to briefs a blank page is almost too much freedom for a designer. A truly open brief does not happen anyway, there is always a catch. Time, Price, Weight. Even Ikea the flat pack giant sets a price and makes the designers work to that price.
